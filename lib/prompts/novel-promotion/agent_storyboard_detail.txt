【Shot Language Library】

**Shot Types**:
- Extreme wide shot: Grand scenes, epic sense, tiny characters
- Wide shot/Full shot: Establish environment, character relationships
- Medium shot: Dialogue, interaction, daily life
- Close-up: Emotion, reaction
- Extreme close-up: Eyes, hands, key props
- Ultra extreme close-up: Pupils, lips, a tear, etc.

**Perspectives**:
- Eye-level: Daily, equal, natural
- Low-angle: Intimidation, sublimity (action/epic scenes)
- High-angle: Smallness, overall view (grand scenes)
- Over-shoulder: Dialogue, confrontation
- Dutch angle: Unease, tension (suspense/tension scenes)
- Subjective perspective: Immersion

**Camera Movements**:
- Fixed: Gaze, silence, daily dialogue
- Slow push/pull: Emotion brewing, revelation, gentle transition
- Follow: Character movement, daily walking
- Rapid push/pull: Shock, impact (tension scenes)
- Orbit/rise/dive: Ceremony sense, epic sense (grand scenes)
- Handheld shake: Chaos, tension (action scenes)

【Select Shot Style Based on scene_type】

**daily (daily/dialogue)**:
- Mainly medium shots, close-ups, occasional extreme close-ups
- Mainly eye-level, alternating over-shoulder shots
- ✅ Prioritize slow push/pull/slight follow, avoid pure fixed shots
- Camera movement words: Slowly push close, gently follow, slight shake, slow orbit
- Character actions: Even in dialogue scenes, add small actions (nod, turn head, gesture, walk)

**emotion (emotional/lyrical)**:
- Close-ups, extreme close-ups capture emotion
- Emotional climax can use ultra extreme close-up
- ✅ Prioritize slow push, orbiting camera movement, avoid pure fixed
- Camera movement words: Slowly push close, gently orbit, slight shake
- Character actions: Lightly raise head, turn around, lower head, raise hand to wipe tears, walk toward window

**action (action/combat)**:
- Rapid shot type switching, extreme close-up + full shot alternating
- Low-angle, high-angle, Dutch angle increase impact
- Rapid push/pull, follow, handheld shake
- Camera movement words: Suddenly, rapidly, quickly, explosive

**epic (epic/grand)**:
- Must have extreme wide shot to establish scale
- High-angle, rise, dive show magnificence
- Characters placed at frame edges to highlight smallness
- Camera movement words: Slowly rise, rapidly dive, orbit

**suspense (suspense/tension)**:
- Subjective perspective, Dutch angle
- Slow push creates pressure
- Sudden switching breaks rhythm

【Shot Continuity Rules】
- Shots must be continuous, cannot have interruptions
- Same group panels need gradual progression: far → medium → close or close → medium → far
- New scenes generally need establishing full shot
- Panels need diversity, don't repeat similar shot types
- Make visuals dynamic, not rigid

【video_prompt Writing Rules - Important】

Video models don't recognize names, must use **age range + gender** to replace:
- Format: Age gender + action + camera movement + environment
- Select dynamic intensity based on scene type
- Prohibited content not in panels
- Involving movement must have dynamics, static must enrich body language and expressions
- If original text is speaking, prompt must clearly state "speaking"

⚠️ 【Dynamic Priority Principle - Core Rule】

Videos cannot be rigid! Each video_prompt must contain "movement" elements:

1. **Character Action Word Bank** (must use):
   - Head: Turn head, nod, raise head, lower head, tilt head, look back
   - Hands: Raise hand, wave, point, clench fist, put down, pick up, touch
   - Body: Walk, turn around, stand up, sit down, lean forward, step back, approach
   - Expression: Lightly frown, corners of mouth rise, eyes flicker, gently smile

2. **Camera Movement Word Bank** (prioritize these, avoid "fixed"):
   - Common: Slowly push close, gently follow, slight shake, orbiting shot
   - Dynamic: Handheld follow, slight shake, slow orbit, rise high-angle
   - Intense: Rapidly push close, quickly follow, suddenly pull far, dive down

3. **Prohibited Pure Static Description**:
   ❌ Wrong: "Young woman sitting on sofa, camera fixed"
   ✅ Correct: "Young woman sitting on sofa gently turns head, camera slowly pushes close to her profile"
   
   ❌ Wrong: "Middle-aged man standing at door, serious expression"
   ✅ Correct: "Middle-aged man pushes door open and enters, lightly frowns, camera handheld follow"

4. **Even dialogue scenes must be dynamic**:
   ❌ Wrong: "Young man speaking, camera fixed"
   ✅ Correct: "Young man speaking while gesturing, gently nods, camera slowly pushes close"

⚠️ **Flashback/narration/inner monologue rules**:
- Prohibited only writing character still contemplating, spacing out, empty shot
- video_prompt must show **actual actions and scenes** in narrative content
- Visuals strongly bound to plot, don't just "character standing recalling"
- Example: Narrative "meeting back then" → Must write actual action visuals when meeting

**Age Range Categories** (only use these terms):
- Boy/girl: Approximately 10-16 years old
- Young man/young woman: Approximately 17-30 years old
- Middle-aged man/middle-aged woman: Approximately 31-50 years old
- Old man/old woman: 50+ years old

⚠️ 【Close-up Shots Must Use Fixed Camera】
- When shot type is "close-up" (e.g., hand close-up, item close-up, partial close-up, etc.)
- video_prompt must clearly write "fixed camera" or "camera fixed still"
- Prohibited any camera movement in close-up shots
- Reason: Close-up visuals only show local area, camera movement will expose other parts

**Examples** (note dynamic elements):
- Daily dialogue: "Young woman picks up coffee cup gently blows, looks up toward window, sunlight on profile, camera slowly pushes close to her silhouette"
- Action scene: "Boy leaps into air swings sword in arc, robes fluttering, camera handheld low-angle follow"
- Emotional scene: "Young woman slowly lowers head, tears slide down cheek, raises hand to wipe corner of eye, camera gently orbits her"
- Dialogue scene: "Middle-aged man taps fingers on table, serious expression speaking, camera slightly shakes shooting"
- Walking scene: "Young man quickly walks on street, wind lifts clothes, camera handheld follow shooting"
- Close-up shot: "One hand slowly turns page, fingertips gently stroke text, fixed camera"
